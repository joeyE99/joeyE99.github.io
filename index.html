About me

I am an undergraduate student major in Condensed Matter Physics at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC). And I am a current member of Zeng Reserch group. Professor Zeng is a Professor of Chemistry at USTC and PI of the Hefei National Laboratory for Physical Sciences at the Microscale (HFNL). We are focusing on atomic-level design of active sites and atomic-level understanding of catalytic mechanisms.

Research

Nanostructured Materials for Heterogeneous Electrocatalysis

Investigation of Bi2Te3 nanoplates for CO2 electroreduction
Advisor: Jie Zeng, PI of the Hefei National Laboratory for Physical Sciences at the Microscale (HFNL)
  • Synthesized Bi2Te3 nanoplates via a solvothermal method.
  • Found Bi2Te3 nanoplates having satisfactory selectivity of CO2 electroreduction to oxalate.
  • Modulated Bi2Te3 nanoplates into the perforated morphology and explored their growth mechanisms.
  • Obtained a higher CO2 electroreduction selectivity to oxalate with the perforated Bi2Te3 nanoplates.

Studing approaches to control the electrocatalytic performance of Bi2Te3 nanoplates
Advisor: Jie Zeng, PI of the Hefei National Laboratory for Physical Sciences at the Microscale (HFNL)
  • Modulated the Fermi level of Bi2Te3 nanoplates through doping different catalytically inactive elements to adjust the performance of CO2 electroreduction.
  • Modulated the Fermi level of Bi2Te3 nanoplates with high temperature annealing to adjust the performance of CO2 electroreduction.

Anchored Bi single atoms for CO2 electroreduction
Advisor: Jie Zeng, PI of the Hefei National Laboratory for Physical Sciences at the Microscale (HFNL)
  • Synthesized Bi single atoms anchored on carbon black via pumping method.
  • Found anchored Bi single atoms having high selectivity of HCOO− in CO2 electroreduction.
  • Studied the mechanics of the higher CO2 electroreduction performance contrast to BiOx clusters on carbon black.
